David Lardner's Career Plan

Systems Administrator

    My ideal job is a System Administrator. The role of a System Administrator can involve a wide number of things such as:
  • Installing and configuring software, hardware and networks.
  • Monitoring system performance and troubleshooting issues.
  • Set up accounts and workstations
  • Upgrade systems with new releases and models
Within 1 year goal 2-5 Year goal 5+ Year goal
  • Complete some certifications that will help me in getting an IT role (e.g. Microsoft Azure Fundamentals or an ITIL certification)
  • Gain an entry level role within the IT industry
  • Progress within my role in the IT industry or use it to gain another position that is closer to my goal.
  • Complete my university studies and use what I have learnt along with my experiences to get a system administrator role.

Current skills, knowledge, and experience

I studied Information Technology for my HSC and have been working on completing the Microsoft Azure Fundamentals certification. I also have plans on completing more certifications to expand my current skill set. I have a good general knowledge of IT and in my role at work I look after most of the IT related items at our site. I previously worked at Dick Smith Electronics for 2 years where I did most of the troubleshooting, fixing basic problems, and setup of computers in the store I worked at.

Skills required or needing development

The System Administrator role requires a Bachelor of Information Technology, Computer Science or Systems Management which is why I am studying the Bachelor of Information Technology. By completing this degree and doing smaller certifications along the way I will be able to increase my knowledge and hopefully gain some experience while doing this. The Bachelor of Information Technology covers off a lot of the things that the System Administrator role does which will mean I will have a sound knowledge to work off when trying to get this role.
